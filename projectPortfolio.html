Project Portfolio

Portfolio is not just a way to present who I am. It’s a way to showcase what I can do as an UX designer. I conducted interviews and survey with professionals in the UX team at Mastercard to have a in-depth understanding of what a professional and attracting UX portfolio should be like and build a portfolio to truly deliver on what the industry needs.


Research

Questionnaire

The purpose of the survey is to find out what a senior UX designer expect to see in a junior UX’s portfolio when hiring. The questionnaire includes questions about content, medium, visua lization, skill set, experience, quantity of projects, and their advices about how to break into the industry. Some UX designers from Mastercard helped to fill out this form using Google Form.

Interview

I’ve interviews 2 senior UX designers who’ve both worked in the field for more than 10 years and currently working at Mastercard. The conversation were mainly about what are the essential elements of a UX design portfolio and how to make my portfolio stand out among other people’s portfolio. I also interviewed with a person who successfully made a transition from marketing to UX design. The questions I had for her were about how she present her portfolio and how she entered this profession without previous related experience.
